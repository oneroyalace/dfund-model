In 2022, DemocracyFund asked, "What would it cost to ensure that everyone in the United States had access to the news and information they need?" They enlisted the help of SeaChange, a non-profit specializing in financial analysis, to build a model estimating the cost of providing local news to every community in the country.

What does the model do?

The Journalism Ecosystem model estimates the cost of fully funding local news ecosystems at the school district, township, city, county, congressional distsrict, and state levels. To estimate that number, it combines the number of these localities in the U.S. with journalism expense numbers. Locality counts are taken from the U.S. Census, and journalism expense numbers are drawn from anonymized data provided by the Institue for Nonprofit News.

What's on this website?

This website showcases two interactive calculators that implement the logic of the model, allowing new users to easily interact with it. The calculators on this website explain how the model arives at its cost estimates, hopefully easing user's paths to directly interacting with and changing the model themselves.

This website also hosts a version of the model available for download.

This website is maintained by the DeWitt Wallace Center for Media & Democracy, part of Duke University's Sanford School for Public Policy.

Calculator 1: Estimate costs for the national news ecosystem

This calculator allows the user to input the minimum desired number of editorial staff per focus per coverage unit. For example, the user could ask that the model calculate the costs of funding local news ecosystems to the point that each coverage unit (e.g., a county or municipality) is covered by an education reporter, a community services reporter, and a criminal justice reporter. The model would then calculate the costs and personnel required to staff newsrooms at that level. The calculator online differs slightly from the model developed by SeaChange in that it allows the user to select the number of editorial employees assigned to each individual type of coverage unit. We made this change to demonstrate how flexible the model’s framework was and how it can be changed to introduce more granularity.

Calculator 2: Estimate costs for a single state’s news ecosystem

This calculator functions much like the national news ecosystem calculator but also allows the user to assign additional reporters to a state based on its population. In addition to being used as a benchmark for statewide journalist ecosystems, the state calculator may provide a useful prototype for researchers hoping to transform the model for use in smaller localities (e.g., counties or cities).

Journalism ecosystem cost calculator instructions

The calculator below uses the model's journalist expense figures and census data to approximate a cost for funding local news ecosystems across the nation.

Try modifying the inputs below to see how different allocations of journalists across localities changes the total cost of the ecosytem. After changing the inputs, makes sure to press "Enter" on your keyboard or click on the "Update calculation" button.

To see how numbers are being calculated, click the "Show calculation" button underneath tne inputs.
